The VendorIQ Story

The team behind VendorIQ represents a collection of unique individuals that share a single trait: love of Main Street businesses and their owners. VendorIQ was created by the Main Street Genome team, and our entire team is made up of small business owners, restauranteurs, come from families of business owners or have grown up working in small businesses.

VendorIQ initially emerged from research by Scott Case, founding CTO of Priceline and the CEO of the Startup America Project, and Eric Koester, a professor of entrepreneurship at Georgetown University and the co-founder of Zaarly.com. Scott and Eric were struck by the fact that small businesses were generating more and more digital data, but weren’t benefiting from that data.

Said cofounder Scott Case, “Main Street businesses are the backbone of our country — and we just felt like someone should be focused on making them more successful. We’re seeing fewer small businesses being started and we want to help reverse that."

Using data to match Restaurants with Main Street Certified Vendors

The team was particularly struck to find that independent restaurants were spending billions on goods and services to run their businesses, but critical decisions about how to select the vendors that supplied them were far from scientific. This created an inefficient buying process costing the independents billions — in fact, our research found independent restaurants paying more than large chains — to the tune of 20-25% higher prices, primarily driven by the inefficient buying patterns of independent owners.

The Main Street Genome team asked itself: could we use technology to help these independents purchase more efficiently — and in return help these owners drive more profit?

After nearly a year of research and development, VendorIQ was born.

VendorIQ works by partnering with the vendors that serve independent businesses, from food and beverage vendors to equipment and banks to cleaning and accounting. The Main Street Genome team uses VendorIQ to match restaurant owners with Main Street Certified Vendors. These vendors are highly rated by independent restaurant owners and have agreed to a first of its kind transparency policy.

VendorIQ uses its leading contributed data platform to analyze millions of transactions in real-time, monitoring market and pricing fluctuations. For years, these sorts of marketplace insights were only available to large franchises and corporate chains who had the scale to monitor across their many locations, but now advances in technology allow our team to bring first-of-its-kind data management and analysis to Main Street.

Partnering With Main Street

The Main Street Genome team has ambitious plans to continue to build data-driven tools to support Main Street owners. From restauranteurs and retailers to doctors and dentists to builders and wholesalers, they each are embracing technology to make their businesses grow and thrive.

Said cofounder Eric Koester, “We’re not going anywhere. My dad was a small business owner and I’ve spent much of my career working with, for and studying the small business economy. I’m so excited to continue helping owners realize their dreams — it keeps me jumping out of bed every day.”

Main Street Genome includes top data scientists, engineers, business development specialists, economists, investors and other leading minds focused on the small business economy. We’re always looking for talented individuals, passionate about Main Street, interested in joining our team — drop us a line at jobs@mainstreetgenome.com.
