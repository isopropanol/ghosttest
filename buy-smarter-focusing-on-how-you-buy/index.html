Buy smarter - Focusing on how you buy

05 January 2015

Turns out that how you buy can have a major impact on your prices.

Three secrets to get better prices Buying at the best price is one of the most important things you can do as a restaurant owner. Of course, just because you are buying, doesnâ€™t mean your vendors are giving you the best price (unless you are growing your own food out back).

Turns out contrary to popular belief vendor pricing is often driven by the way you buy. Here are the three quickest ways to reduce your pricing.

  1. Stop Breaking cases - Vendors may charge owners who consistently break cases 20-45% more for product to compensate for housing un-sold product

  2. Eliminate multiple deliveries in a day - Getting multiple deliveries on the same day (excluding those for bad product) is one of the fastest ways to get higher pricing from your vendors

  3. Drop Sizes - Increasing your drop size by reducing the trips you make to Costco, Restaurant Depot, etc. can lead to lower pricing as you become a more valuable and manageable customer for your vendor.
