Data playing a larger role in selecting vendors

05 January 2015

Restaurant owners are arming themselves to make better choices

A vendor’s NPS (Net Promoter Score) – the willingness of their own clients to recommend them to another owner can reveal both the most attentive suppliers and those serious issues. When it comes down to it, it’s more than a numbers game, and the relationship between owner and vendor can reach a point where both need to part ways, as relationship has become either efficient or otherwise toxic.

In an attempt to deal this issue, owners are arming themselves with the confidence and tools needed to make the sometimes necessary switch to a vendor willing to be transparent and best suited their needs. When restaurant owners recognize their own value, they are empowered to seek out a stronger vendor partnership.

We know that most owners have experienced at least 1 of the following:

An upsurge in items without notification
Dissatisfaction with customer service
A significant drop in quality and product consistency
Inconsistent delivery schedules
Opportunities to access the same products for a lower price 

By getting access to increased market transparency through trusted third parties owners are increasingly aware of their opportunities. With data in their hands, these choices become straightforward business decisions, and owners can be clear about how and where they are saving.

While loyalty to a vendor or vendor rep has always been an important part of the restaurant business, increased access to transparent marketplace data and Main Street Certified Vendors are changing owners' calculus. The fact is, prices, service and product quality have the most significant impact on an owner's final decisions about which vendors get their valuable business. When these variables are known quantities the prospect of selecting a vendor is a matter of straightforward analysis, leaving the drama out of the equation.

Cameron Nutting
